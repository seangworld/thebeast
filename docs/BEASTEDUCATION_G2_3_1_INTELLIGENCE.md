# BeastEducation G2.3.1 Intelligence

G2.3.1 improves the existing Guidance Counselor's long-term advising quality. It does not rename the product, replace onboarding, or rebuild roadmap and teaching functionality.

The intelligence layer progressively assembles five answers:

1. Who the user is.
2. Where the user is now.
3. Where the user wants to go.
4. What meaningfully changed.
5. What the best next step is and why.

## Capabilities

- Adaptive interviewing asks no more than three high-value questions at a time.
- Profile refinements separate stated or observed facts from unsupported inference.
- Career and certification guidance explains evidence, prerequisites, uncertainty, and verification needs.
- Opportunity detection accepts permissioned cross-module signals without copying source-module business logic.
- Milestone recognition uses roadmap status and meaningful progress evidence.
- Recommendations include reason, explanation, confidence, evidence IDs, and verification boundaries.
- Long-term continuity is stored through owner-scoped BeastAgents memory records.
- BeastAgents Context Assembly and Event Bus remain the shared integration boundary.
- Approved playbooks may add recommendations when available; deterministic guidance remains useful without a playbook.

## Boundaries

- No autonomous enrollment, application, scheduling, purchasing, or credential decisions.
- No fabricated profile facts, progress, requirements, opportunities, deadlines, costs, or eligibility.
- Cross-module context remains permissioned, purpose-limited, and source-owned.
- Teaching remains with specialist resources and tutoring capabilities.
